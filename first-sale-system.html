The Story

This Book Exists Because I Was
Sitting in the Wrong Seat

I was an account clerk. The job paid the bills. It was stable, predictable, and it took something from me every single day. I started mixing foot scrubs and foot soaks in my kitchen — less than J$5,000 in ingredients — not as a business plan, but as something extra I could offer my nail clients. Just to make their experience better. That small, practical thing planted something I couldn't ignore.

I moved into Yoni care. My very first Yoni Oil sale came back as a complaint. The client didn't like it. Most people stop there — they take that as a sign that it's not for them. I went back to my kitchen, reformulated, and came back. She loved it. The products took off. Women started saying my hands were blessed. I don't know if that's true, but I know I didn't stop when it was easy to stop.

I sat at that accounting desk for over a year after I knew it was time to leave. Not because I wasn't ready. Because leaving felt terrifying. You build up a picture in your head of what could go wrong — and it feels more real than what could go right. Until the day the discomfort of staying becomes louder than the fear of going. That's when you move.

"You will know when it's time to leave. Not because of logic. Because of how wrong it feels to stay."
