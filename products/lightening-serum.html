About this product

What is the Najah Chemist Lightening Serum?

The Najah Chemist Lightening Serum is a concentrated multi-active treatment targeting dark spots, hyperpigmentation, and uneven skin tone. Four proven brightening actives work through different pathways simultaneously — Kojic Acid inhibits tyrosinase, Alpha Arbutin blocks melanin synthesis at the cellular level, Niacinamide interrupts melanin transfer to skin cells, and Vitamin C neutralises oxidative stress that triggers pigmentation. Together they produce a broader and faster brightening effect than any single active alone.

A serum delivers these actives at higher concentrations than a moisturiser, with a lightweight texture that penetrates quickly into the skin. Applied before moisturiser in the skincare routine, it forms the active treatment layer that drives visible results. Licorice Root Extract and Lactic Acid provide additional gentle brightening and exfoliation support.

Kojic Acid
Kojic Acid

Inhibits tyrosinase — the key enzyme in melanin production. Reduces the formation of new dark spots and progressively fades existing hyperpigmentation. One of the most clinically validated skin brightening ingredients in dermatology.

Alpha Arbutin
Alpha-Arbutin

A stable, skin-safe form of arbutin that inhibits melanin synthesis at the cellular level. More effective and less irritating than hydroquinone. Works on a different pathway to Kojic Acid — combined they produce synergistic brightening.

Niacinamide
Niacinamide (Vitamin B3)

Interrupts the transfer of melanin from melanocytes to skin surface cells — preventing dark spots from surfacing even when they form beneath. Also reduces redness, strengthens the skin barrier, and improves overall skin texture.

Vitamin C
Ascorbic Acid

Potent antioxidant that neutralises free radicals triggered by UV exposure — one of the primary causes of hyperpigmentation. Also directly inhibits melanin production. Works synergistically with all other brightening actives in this formula.

How to use

Usage instructions

1
Cleanse and tone the face first. The serum should be applied to clean skin — any residue or barrier from previous products reduces penetration.
2
Apply 3–4 drops to the face. Dot across forehead, both cheeks, nose, and chin. The serum is concentrated — a little covers the full face.
3
Pat gently into the skin using fingertips. Do not rub — patting encourages better absorption of the active ingredients without mechanical friction.
4
Allow 1–2 minutes to absorb fully before applying moisturiser on top. Layering moisturiser seals in the actives and adds hydration.
5
Always use SPF 30+ during the day. Brightening actives make the skin more photosensitive, and sun exposure will reverse brightening progress. SPF is non-negotiable with this product.

FAQ

Common questions

How does a serum differ from a moisturiser?
A serum is a concentrated active treatment; a moisturiser is a hydration and protection layer.Serums have a higher concentration of active ingredients in a lightweight, fast-absorbing base. They are applied before moisturiser and are specifically designed to deliver actives into the skin, not to sit on the surface.
How long before I see results?
4–8 weeks for visible improvement, with daily SPF use.Brightening serums require consistent daily use and SPF protection to show results. Without SPF, UV exposure continues to trigger new pigmentation and reverses progress. Set your retail customers' expectations accordingly.
