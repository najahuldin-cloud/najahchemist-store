What this formula targets

How dark spot serums actually work

This is a results-driven brightening formula built on active ingredients — not a homemade or all-natural product. Here is the science behind it.

Full INCI: Water, Glycerin, Aloe Vera, Propylene Glycol, Stearic Acid, Cetearyl Alcohol, Cetyl Alcohol, Kojic Acid, Turmeric Oil, Organic Herbal Extract, Salicylic Acid, Lactic Acid, Vitamin E, Xanthan Gum, Fragrance, Preservatives, EDTA

🔬
Melanin Inhibition
Dark spots form when melanin overproduces in response to sun damage, acne, or hormonal changes. A corrector serum works by targeting the enzyme tyrosinase, which controls melanin production. By interrupting this process with brightening actives, the serum gradually fades existing dark spots and helps prevent new ones from forming with continued use.
💧
Leave-On Delivery
Serums are leave-on formulas — they stay on the skin after application. This gives the active ingredients extended contact time compared to a rinse-off cleanser or soap. The lightweight serum texture allows faster absorption, meaning the brightening actives penetrate into the skin layers where melanin production occurs rather than sitting on the surface.
Formulation note: This serum is produced by Najah Chemist using carefully selected active ingredients. It is a professional-grade formula — not a homemade product and not positioned as all-natural. It is designed for brands building results-focused brightening product lines in Jamaica and the Caribbean.

How is the Dark Spot Corrector Serum different from a cream?
A serum is a lightweight, fast-absorbing liquid formula with a higher concentration of active ingredients than a typical cream. It is designed to penetrate into the skin quickly to deliver brightening actives to the layers where melanin production occurs. A cream is thicker, sits closer to the surface, and provides more moisturisation. In a brightening routine, the serum is used as the treatment step before a moisturiser or cream.

Many brands stock both for customers at different stages of their brightening routine.
